# CodeAlpha Data Science Internship

This repository contains the core projects completed during my Data Science internship at CodeAlpha. The focus of these tasks was on implementing predictive modeling using regression analysis, exploring time-series data, and applying ensemble learning techniques to extract actionable business insights.

**Core Tech Stack:** Python, Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-Learn, Matplotlib, Seaborn

---

## Task 1: Car Price Prediction
**Objective:** Develop a machine learning pipeline to predict the market value of used cars based on temporal and categorical vehicle specifications.
* **Algorithm:** Random Forest Regressor
* **Key Techniques:** Feature Engineering (absolute vehicle age derivation), One-Hot Encoding (bypassing the dummy variable trap), and robust data cleaning.
* **Libraries:** `scikit-learn`, `pandas`, `seaborn`
* **Results:** Achieved a highly accurate predictive model with an R-squared score of **0.9595** and a Mean Absolute Error (MAE) of **0.63**, providing a reliable baseline for automated vehicle valuation.

## Task 2: Unemployment Analysis in India
**Objective:** Analyze the socioeconomic impact of the 2020 global pandemic and subsequent lockdowns on unemployment rates across different Indian states.
* **Methodology:** Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and Time-Series Tracking
* **Key Techniques:** Temporal data visualization, regional variance mapping, and handling missing data across irregular time intervals.
* **Libraries:** `pandas`, `matplotlib`, `seaborn`
* **Results:** Delivered clear, data-driven visual insights into localized labor market volatility, successfully highlighting the most heavily impacted regions during peak lockdown months.

## Task 3: Advertising Sales Prediction
**Objective:** Analyze the impact of advertising expenditures across multiple channels (TV, Radio, and Newspaper) to forecast total sales and optimize marketing budget allocation.
* **Algorithm:** Linear Regression
* **Key Techniques:** Correlation matrix mapping, Linearity testing, and regression coefficient interpretation.
* **Libraries:** `scikit-learn`, `pandas`, `seaborn`
* **Results:** Achieved an R-squared score of **0.9059**, mathematically identifying TV advertising as the primary revenue driver and demonstrating that newspaper spend yielded negligible ROI for the given dataset.
